# Friends Mode

Join a minecraft server on any console with the friend mode.
## Before you start

- Add the **MCCompanion friend account** on your console and make sure the friend request is accepted

---

## Steps

1. **Connect everything to the same network**

Make sure your phone/tablet running MCCompanion and your console are on the **same Wi‑Fi** *(avoid Guest Wi‑Fi if possible)* while using MCCompanion Friends.

2. **Open MCCompanion**

Launch the app on your phone or tablet.

3. **Enter your server details**

Enter the **server address** *(IP or domain)* and **port** *(default: **19132**)*, or pick one from your saved/recommended servers.

4. **Select a server and select Friends as the mode**

Tap on the server you prefer and select **Friends**.

5. **Start Friends Mode**

Tap **Start Friends Mode**. MCCompanion will send the configuration to its servers.

6. **Add the bot as a friend on the Xbox network**

Copy the **gamertag** shown in the MCCompanion app and add it as a friend on the **Xbox network**.

7. **Join from your console**

Open **Minecraft** on your console and navigate to the friends section. You should see the user you added. Join their game.

---

## Not working?

A few things to try:
- Your phone/tablet and console MUST be on the same WiFi
- Try refreshing the Friends tab; it doesn't always update on its own
- Restart Minecraft and enable Friends mode again before rejoining
- Double-check that the MCCompanion friend account is accepted, not just added
- Xbox requires an online multiplayer subscription, PlayStation requires PlayStation Plus, and Nintendo Switch requires Nintendo Switch Online, to connect to online multiplayer servers
- Double-check the IP and port (default: 19132)
- Make sure the server you are trying to connect to allows Bedrock players to connect

---

:::tip
If your friends can see the world but can't join, it's usually a NAT issue on their side. Ask them to check their network settings or try a different connection.
:::
